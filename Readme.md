##  Project Overview

This project performs customer segmentation on an e-commerce dataset using **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Analysis** and **K-Means Clustering**. The objective is to identify valuable customer groups and provide actionable business recommendations for customer retention, growth, and churn reduction.

The project follows an end-to-end analytics workflow, including data cleaning, feature engineering, clustering, customer profiling, and dashboard development in Power BI.

---

## Business Problem

Businesses often apply the same marketing strategy to all customers, resulting in inefficient campaigns and lower ROI.

This project helps answer:

- Who are the most valuable customers?
- Which customers are at risk of churning?
- Which customers should be targeted for upselling?
- How can marketing campaigns be personalized for different customer groups?

##  Customer Segments

###  VIP Customers
- Highest spending customers
- Highest purchase frequency
- Most recent buyers

###  Loyal Customers
- Consistent repeat buyers
- Strong revenue contributors
- High upgrade potential

###  Potential Customers
- Recently active customers
- Low purchase frequency
- Opportunity for growth

###  At-Risk Customers
- Long inactive period
- Low engagement
- High churn probability

## Key Business Insights

- VIP customers represent only **14.6%** of customers while contributing approximately **61%** of total revenue.
- Loyal customers present the highest opportunity for revenue growth through upselling.
- At-Risk customers require immediate retention campaigns.
- Potential customers can be converted into Loyal customers through personalized marketing.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Launch exclusive loyalty programs for VIP customers.
- Upsell Loyal customers using personalized offers.
- Increase repeat purchases among Potential customers.
- Run win-back campaigns for At-Risk customers.
